# AI 201a Project 4: Provenance Guard

## Problem Statement:
The websites where we share and enjoy original writing, music, and art are dealing with a tough new question: how do we know if a piece was actually made by a human, or just generated by AI? The goal isn't to ruin the fun or ban new tech. It's simply about protecting real creators, building trust, and giving audiences the honest context they deserve.

## Detection signals:

### Detection Signal 1: LLM-based Classification (Semantics, Tone, Context)
Will ask the model to assess whether the text reads as human or AI generated by capturing semantic and stylistic coherence holistically in the system prompt and return a dict containing the following output. 
Output: Dictionary containing transparency label, llm confidence score, normalized user text

### Detection Signal 2: Stylometric heuristics (Word Structure)
Will compute the following properties using pure python:

Sentence Length Variance: AI writing tends to be more uniform. Human writing is more variable. Low variance is a signal

Type-Token Ratio: unique words / total words, AI text repeats common phrases; human writing is more lexically diverse.

Punctuation Variation: AI text has lower punctuation variation per sentence; human writing, especially informal — uses it expressively.

Output: Dictionary containing classification, stylometric confidence score, raw text

### Combining Confidence Scores  
I will take average confidence scores: LLM_score + Stylometric_score / 2 \
I will round the average to 2 decimal places

## Appeals workflow:
<!-- 
Who can submit an appeal? What information do they provide? What does the system do when an appeal is received — what status changes, what gets logged? What would a human reviewer see when they open the appeal queue?
-->
An author who believes their piece of text was misclassified can appeal. They will provide their appeal reason. When an appeal is received, the system records the creator's explanation, changes the content status from "reviewed" to "under_review," and creates an audit log entry documenting the appeal. The original attribution decision remains unchanged to preserve the audit trail. Human reviewers can view the original classification, individual signal scores, confidence score, submitted content, and the creator's explanation when reviewing the appeal queue.

AI patterns
